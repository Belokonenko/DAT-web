General in the Field

General in the Field. Pre-emergence herbicide for controlling annual broadleaf and some grass weeds in canola and other crops.

  • Active Ingredient:

    Clomazone, 480 g/L

  • Formulation:

    Emulsifiable concentrate

  • Usage Rate:

    0.2-0.6 L/ha

  • Toxicity Class:

    2

  • Shelf Life:

    2 years

Characteristics:

Systemic preparation enters the plant through the hypocotyl and root system, moving through the xylem to the leaves;

By penetrating the plant, it stops the formation of chlorophyll and carotene, thus halting the photosynthesis process;

Destroys weeds and creates conditions for good growth and development of winter canola in the autumn period, eliminating competition for light, moisture, and nutrients at the earliest stages of organogenesis;

Well-bound in soil and minimally leachable;

Commonly used for comprehensive protection of canola in tank mixes with the active ingredients metazachlor and propizochlor;

In Eastern European countries (Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland), the use of Command® on canola crops is an essential element in the cultivation technology;

Widely used in more than 100 countries worldwide on crops such as canola, soybeans, tobacco, peppers,carrots,potatoes, melons, cotton, and other crops.

Recommendations:

For maximum effect, quality soil preparation is required: no clods, a leveled field surface, and no plant residues;

Soil spraying should be carried out within two days, preferably immediately after sowing;

Soil spraying should be carried out within two days, preferably immediately after sowing;

In case of insufficient soil moisture after spraying, it is recommended to roll the soil with ring-spiked rollers;

The application rate depends on the humus content in the soil; it can be slightly reduced on low-humus soils; Whitening of the first pair of canola leaves may occur, but this will disappear over time, and plants will have a more developed root system and better withstand low temperatures;

If re-sowing is necessary, canola and soybeans can be sown 30-60 days after treatment after shallow tillage to a dep th of 8-10 cm; for all other crops, deep plowing is recommended;

Ensuring complete coverage of the field surface is a mandatory requirement when applying the preparation.
